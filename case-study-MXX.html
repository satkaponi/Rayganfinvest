Case study - Ampersand

Start-up : E-Commerce

The Vibgyor Chain of schools had grown big and the promoter wanted to diversify into new businesses. As Group President/Advisor, I first established a Group entity , “Ampersand Group” , revisited and expanded their Vision, reformulated their strategy and zeroed on a few new or adjacent business ideas we planned to foray into. One of the ideas was an e-commerce startup offering school supplies. The Vibgyor Schools were already selling text books, uniforms as well as accessories like shoes and sports ware to their students. We built on this idea. There have been several companies in this space, but no one was very successful . We tried to identify the reasons. We knew this market was there, it was sizable and the trends indicated more towards on-line buying as parents had led time to make visits to schools to pick up the text books, uniforms etc. from the school themselves.

While we had a sizable captive mass of Vibgyor children as potential customers, we had to reach out to all other students to achieve meaningful revenues. We approached the problem differently. We offered to different chains of schools a white labelled web site where their captive children could buy stuff online, ostensibly from their own school’s online portal. The back-end and the entire supply chain, was of course handled by us. This was a unique idea. We were able to ramp up pretty quickly. Now with the threat of the pandemic, this has turned out to be and even higher potential idea. Within one year we were able to start the pilots and in the second year we went beyond our own schools and were able to sign up with close to 5 school chains. The lockdown slowed downs things a bit but in the longer term this abodes well for us.
